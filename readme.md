### Recommended Reading Approach (by AI)
Based on the traditional progression and the search results, here's a recommended approach to studying Hindu texts systematically:

1. Begin with Foundational Texts
Start with one or more of these accessible entry points:

`Bhagavad Gita` - This dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna provides a concise summary of Hindu philosophy and is more accessible than the Vedas for most beginners

`Principal Upanishads` - Beginning with the Mandukya Upanishad as suggested in the Muktika, followed by other principal Upanishads

2. Explore the Epics for Context

`Ramayana` and selections from the `Mahabharat` provide narrative context and practical applications of Hindu philosophy

3. Study the Vedic Samhitas
Begin with selections from the `Rigved`, particularly hymns addressing cosmology and metaphysics

Progress to selections from other Vedas based on your specific interests

4. Deepen Understanding Through Commentaries
Brahmanas and Aranyakas provide deeper context for understanding Vedic rituals and their symbolic meanings, basically podcasts, teachings of Gurus (e.g. Sadhguru)
